The Intersection of Deep Learning and Differential Equations

Published on October 26, 2024 by Rohit Udaiwal

A PINN is a neural network that is trained to solve two things simultaneously: fit the observed data, and ensure the output obeys the governing physical laws. The magic is in the loss function.

Let's consider a simple ODE, for example, the equation for exponential decay: $$ \frac{du}{dt} = -\lambda u $$ Our total loss function, $\mathcal{L}$, would be a combination of the data loss ($\mathcal{L}_{data}$) and the physics loss ($\mathcal{L}_{physics}$): $$ \mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{data} + \mathcal{L}_{physics} $$

This approach allows us to incorporate domain knowledge directly into the learning process, making the model more interpretable and potentially more accurate with limited data. The physics loss ensures that the neural network's predictions are consistent with the known mathematical relationships that govern the system.
